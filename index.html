There are several subspecies of tigers, each adapted to different habitats:

  • Bengal Tiger - Found in India, Bangladesh, Nepal; most numerous.
  • Siberian (Amur) Tiger - Largest subspecies, lives in cold Russian forests.
  • Indochinese Tiger - Southeast Asia's elusive jungle dweller.
  • Malayan Tiger - Found in the Malay Peninsula.
  • South China Tiger - Critically endangered, possibly extinct in the wild.
  • Sumatran Tiger - Smallest subspecies, lives in Indonesia's rainforests.
  • Extinct subspecies include the Javan, bali, and Caspian tigers.
